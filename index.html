BINSAT Hardware & Simulation Experiments

This page showcases the experimental results of the BINSAT hierarchical motion planner. The videos below demonstrate a 16-DoF snake robot executing the "multi-reaching" behavior to navigate highly constrained environments and avoid spatial obstacles.

Hardware Validation: The first featured video presents our physical hardware experiment, where the snake robot successfully performs two sequential reaching behaviors.

Simulation Runs: The subsequent software videos display 8 simulation runs conducted across 4 distinct environments. Each environment consists of a uniquely oriented set of tunnels. To demonstrate the planner's robustness, each of the 4 environments includes 2 separate runs featuring random spatial displacements of the tunnels.

Hardware Validation: Multi-Reaching

Physical hardware experiment demonstrating the full sequence: the robot anchors its base, aligns its head with the first tunnel, and successfully executes the anchoring behavior inside the tight space before reaching for the final target.
